Community Guide

Explore Charlotte
Communities

Every neighborhood tells a different story. From lakefront living on Lake Norman to the tree-lined streets of South Charlotte, from the charm of historic Waxhaw to the energy of new construction in Fort Mill, Charlotte offers a community for every lifestyle.

The right neighborhood makes all the difference in how you experience home. Use this guide to compare the area's most desirable communities across North and South Carolina.

South Charlotte & Union County

Elegant suburbs and established communities

Ballantyne

A thriving community featuring business parks, championship golf courses, excellent schools, and a mix of single-family homes, townhomes, and apartments. Convenient shopping and dining at Ballantyne Village.

South Charlotte

Encompasses established neighborhoods like Stonecrest, Lansdowne, and Providence Plantation. Known for top-rated schools, convenient highway access, and proximity to SouthPark shopping.

Weddington

Highly sought-after Union County town known for spacious lots, excellent schools, and a family-oriented community atmosphere.

Waxhaw

Historic downtown charm meets modern growth. Highly-rated Union County schools, expanding residential developments, and a strong sense of community.

Marvin

Small Union County town offering large lots, rural character, and proximity to both Charlotte and the growing areas of Waxhaw and Weddington.

Pineville

South Charlotte area with convenient I-77 and I-485 access, Carolina Place Mall, and diverse housing options from apartments to single-family homes.

Fort Mill & York County (South Carolina)

Border-town living with easy Charlotte access

Fort Mill

Just across the South Carolina border, Fort Mill offers excellent Fort Mill Schools, new construction neighborhoods, a charming downtown, and easy access to Charlotte. Popular with families and professionals.

York

Historic York County seat offering small-town atmosphere, affordable housing, and proximity to Charlotte while maintaining a distinct community identity.

Tega Cay

Waterfront community on Lake Wylie featuring a peninsula setting, resort-style amenities, golf course, and strong property values.

Lake Wylie and Clover

Growing South Carolina town in York County offering newer developments, Clover School District, and a mix of suburban and rural living.

Rock Hill

York County's largest city featuring Winthrop University, a revitalized downtown, diverse housing options, and a lower cost of living while staying connected to Charlotte.

Southeast Charlotte & Matthews

Historic charm with modern convenience

Matthews

Historic downtown with local shops and restaurants, community festivals, and diverse neighborhoods. Convenient to Uptown Charlotte with a small-town feel.

Mint Hill

Southeast Charlotte area known for family-friendly neighborhoods, good schools, and convenient access to I-485 and major employment centers.

Stallings

Union County town adjacent to Matthews, offering newer developments, good schools, and growing retail and dining options.

Lake Norman Area

Waterfront living and vibrant downtowns

Cornelius

Lakeside town on Lake Norman's southern shore featuring waterfront properties, marinas, walkable downtown, and a growing restaurant scene.

Davidson

Charming college town home to Davidson College. Walkable Main Street, historic architecture, lake access, and strong community engagement.

Huntersville

One of Charlotte's fastest-growing suburbs on Lake Norman. Excellent schools, Birkdale Village shopping, new construction, and easy I-77 access.

Mooresville

Located on Lake Norman's northern shore. Known as "Race City USA" for NASCAR connections. Offers waterfront living, good schools, and a historic downtown.

Troutman

Smaller Iredell County town north of Mooresville offering more affordable Lake Norman area living with a small-town atmosphere.

North & Northeast Charlotte

Growth corridors and established neighborhoods

University City

Home to UNC Charlotte. Diverse neighborhoods, growing retail and dining, and convenient access to I-85 and I-485. Mix of student housing and family neighborhoods.

Harrisburg

Rapidly growing Cabarrus County town northeast of Charlotte. New construction communities, highly-rated schools, and family-friendly atmosphere.

Concord

Cabarrus County seat and home to Charlotte Motor Speedway. Growing residential areas, historic downtown, and convenient access to Charlotte Douglas Airport.

Kannapolis

Cabarrus County town experiencing downtown revitalization. New developments, the North Carolina Research Campus, and affordable housing options.

West Charlotte & Gaston County

Riverside towns and value-driven living

Gastonia

Gaston County seat west of Charlotte offering affordable housing, historic downtown, access to outdoor recreation, and a mix of suburban and rural areas.

Belmont

Historic town along the Catawba River featuring a charming downtown, Daniel Stowe Botanical Garden proximity, and growing residential areas.

Mount Holly

Gaston County town on the Catawba River offering small-town atmosphere, Lake Wylie access, and more affordable housing options.

Cramerton

Small Gaston County town known for its historic mill village character, tree-lined streets, and proximity to the Catawba River.

Lowell

Smaller Gaston County community offering affordable housing and convenient access to Gastonia and Charlotte.

Lancaster County (South Carolina)

Quiet country living with city proximity

Lancaster

South Carolina county seat offering historic downtown, affordable housing, and proximity to Charlotte while maintaining a distinct small-town character.

Pageland

Rural Chesterfield/Lancaster County community offering affordable land, agricultural character, and a quieter pace of life.
